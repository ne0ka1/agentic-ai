Adjusting the carburetor on a Ford Model T involves a few key steps, given the unique characteristics of its system. The Model T uses a simple, low-speed Schebler carburetor that requires basic adjustments for optimal operation. Here’s a general guide on how to adjust it:

### Tools You'll Need:
- Screwdriver
- Wrench or pliers (if needed)

### Steps to Adjust the Carburetor:

1. **Warm Up the Engine**:
   - Before making adjustments, start the engine and let it warm up. A warm engine will provide a more accurate setting.

2. **Locate the Carburetor**:
   - The carburetor is typically located on the side of the engine. Familiarize yourself with the adjustment screws.

3. **Adjust the Low-Speed Mixture**:
   - Identify the low-speed adjustment screw (usually located on the side of the carburetor). 
   - Turn the screw clockwise to enrich the mixture (more fuel) or counterclockwise to lean it out (less fuel).
   - Start by turning the screw 1/4 turn in one direction and listen to the engine. A rich mixture will cause the engine to run smoother but may produce black smoke, while a lean mixture tends to cause rough idle or backfiring.
   - Adjust until the engine runs smoothly at low speeds.

4. **Adjust the High-Speed Mixture**:
   - For this adjustment, you’ll typically want to drive the car to assess performance.
   - While driving, start with the high-speed adjustment screw and make small adjustments (1/8 turn) in either direction, observing how the engine responds under load.
   - A properly adjusted high-speed mixture will provide good performance without backfiring or sputtering.

5. **Adjust the Throttle**:
   - If needed, adjust the throttle linkage to ensure it opens fully when the accelerator pedal is pressed. There might be a small screw or lever that controls the throttle cable.

6. **Check the Idle Speed**:
   - Once the mixture is set, adjust the idle speed using the throttle stop screw. This screw controls how much air/fuel mixture is allowed into the engine at idle.
   - Set it to a comfortable idle speed where the engine runs smoothly without stalling.

7. **Final Testing**:
   - After adjustments, test drive the vehicle at various speeds to ensure it accelerates smoothly and does not stall.
   - Reassess both mixture adjustments if necessary after driving for some time.

### Additional Tips:
- Note that environmental factors (temperature, altitude) can affect carburetor performance, so adjustments might need to be revised under different conditions.
- Take notes of your adjustments so you can revert if necessary.
- If problems persist after adjustments, consider checking for other issues like fuel supply, air leaks, or ignition problems.

Remember, patience is key when adjusting a carburetor. Make small changes and allow the engine to settle before making further adjustments.
